- About me :

It all started when I inherited an HP Vectra 500 from my father at the ripe age of five. I remember as a child watching my dad play computer games, and was impressed with the possibility of being able to create something like that on my own. Like every child of the 90’s generation, I began to play around with Microsoft Paint, drawing everything from trees, people, and military tanks and weapons, to later using Microsoft Publisher to create “homemade magazines.”

After that, I started to gain an interest in hacking video games, which lead me into romhacking at the age of 13. That was the time where I learned the most about how computers work. After romhacking, I started coding my own home-brew apps in LUA for the Sony PSP, which is when I learned C. Unlike most 14 year olds at time, who were busy playing sports and socializing, I was that kid who rushed home from school to continue to learn to program. Now, 20 years later after receiving that first computer, I am an IT professional who specializes in web design and UX, based in Valladolid, Spain. I have completed over 40 projects and worked for many companies, including Adidas (through Alten), El Corte Inglés (The biggest retail establishment in Spain and one of the biggest in Portugal), and Covergreen, among others. Through all of that experience, I strengthened my skills in LUA, C, VB.net, JavaScript, PHP, Python, HTML5, CSS3 and Photoshop.

All thanks to my dad, his computer games, and that HP Vectra 500.
